About the Project

The goal of this project is to make visible the stories and social histories of places within Chinatown, DC, that can help us identify and prioritize projects that will contribute to place-keeping in the neighborhood.

Rising costs of living and commercial redevelopment are overshadowing the neighborhood's cultural assets.The consequence is that aging residents are experiencing increased alienation from their neighborhood and younger generations across the Washington, D.C. metropolitan area are losing an important cultural touchstone in the city.

The Audience

This project is also a thank you to Chinatown's longtime community leaders, past and present residents, and deeply connected family and friends who have built and sustained the cultural life of the neighborhood for decades. This project is to honor these individuals for their steadfast work, caretaking, and leadership.

This space is also intended to be a public educational tool. We envision this project to be in conversation amongst all of the stakeholders that are involved in planning and designing policy and projects that shape how our cities fulfill our everyday needs.

The Process

We consider this website a first edition and hope to see this map build and grow to capture the myriad of experiencesand perspectives of different community groups that have been made invisible over the decades as a result of the on-going gentrification processes.

This project incorporates content from on-going public history projects, the Chinatown Off H Street oral history project and Dear Chinatown, DC's participatory art project, where we conducted interviews, surveys, and poster-making workshops to make visible and tangible beloved neighborhood assets.
